Nurse Loan Forgiveness Programs

As a registered nurse pursuing or holding a BSN degree, you may qualify for various loan forgiveness and repayment programs. These programs can forgive thousands to tens of thousands of dollars in student loan debt in exchange for working in underserved areas or specific healthcare settings.

Federal Loan Forgiveness Programs

1. Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

Program Overview: Forgives remaining federal student loan balance after 120 qualifying monthly payments while working full-time for a qualifying employer.

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Work full-time (30+ hours/week) for a qualifying employer:
    • Government organizations (federal, state, local, tribal)
    • Not-for-profit organizations with 501(c)(3) status
    • Other not-for-profit organizations providing qualifying public services
  • Have federal Direct Loans (or consolidate other federal loans into Direct Loans)
  • Make 120 qualifying monthly payments under an income-driven repayment plan
  • Be employed by a qualifying employer at the time of forgiveness

Qualifying Healthcare Employers:

  • Public hospitals and health systems
  • Veterans Affairs (VA) facilities
  • Indian Health Service facilities
  • Non-profit hospitals (check 501(c)(3) status)
  • Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs)
  • State and county health departments

Forgiveness Amount: 100% of remaining loan balance after 120 payments

Apply At: StudentAid.gov/PSLF

Important: Submit an Employment Certification Form (ECF) annually to track your progress toward forgiveness. Don't wait until you've made all 120 payments!

2. NURSE Corps Loan Repayment Program

Program Overview: Provides up to 85% loan repayment for nurses working in Critical Shortage Facilities (CSFs).

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Licensed RN with minimum of associate degree in nursing
  • U.S. citizen or national
  • No federal service obligation or breach of contract
  • Commit to working full-time (minimum 32 hours/week) at an approved CSF
  • Unpaid qualifying nursing educational loan debt

Award Amounts:

  • 2-year commitment: Up to 60% of unpaid nursing education debt
  • 1-year extension: Additional 25% of original balance
  • Total possible: Up to 85% loan repayment over 3 years

Apply At: HRSA Nurse Corps

Application Period: Typically opens January-March each year

3. National Health Service Corps (NHSC)

Program Overview: Loan repayment for healthcare providers working in Health Professional Shortage Areas (HPSAs).

For Nurses:

  • Primarily for Nurse Practitioners, Certified Nurse-Midwives, and Psychiatric Nurse Specialists
  • RNs may qualify in certain circumstances at NHSC-approved sites
  • Up to $50,000 for 2-year commitment
  • Additional years available for continued service

More Information: NHSC.hrsa.gov

Military Nurse Loan Repayment Programs

U.S. Army Nurse Corps Loan Repayment

  • Up to $120,000 in loan repayment
  • 3-year active duty service commitment
  • Must be commissioned officer
  • Contact: Army AMEDD Recruiting

U.S. Navy Nurse Corps Loan Repayment

  • Up to $65,000 over 3 years
  • Must serve as commissioned officer
  • Contact: Navy Nurse Corps

U.S. Air Force Nurse Transition Program

  • Up to $60,000 in loan repayment
  • Must commission and serve on active duty
  • Contact: Air Force Healthcare

Employer-Based Loan Repayment Programs

Many healthcare employers offer loan repayment as a recruitment and retention benefit:

Hospital Systems and Health Networks:

  • Rural Hospitals: Often offer $5,000-$20,000 in loan repayment over 2-3 years
  • Critical Access Hospitals: May provide substantial repayment packages
  • Major Health Systems: Some offer loan repayment for hard-to-fill specialty positions
  • Veterans Affairs: VA facilities may offer Education Debt Reduction Program (EDRP) up to $200,000 over 5 years for hard-to-recruit positions

How to Find Employers Offering Loan Repayment:

  1. Review job postings carefully for loan repayment benefits
  2. Ask during interviews about student loan assistance programs
  3. Check with rural and underserved area hospitals
  4. Inquire about sign-on bonuses that can be used for loan repayment
  5. Consider Critical Shortage Facilities eligible for NURSE Corps

Tax Note: Under the Consolidated Appropriations Act, employer student loan repayment assistance (up to $5,250 annually) is tax-free through December 31, 2025.

Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) Forgiveness

While not specific to nurses, all federal student loan borrowers may qualify for forgiveness after 20-25 years on income-driven repayment plans:

Available Plans:

  • SAVE Plan: 10% of discretionary income; forgiveness after 20-25 years
  • PAYE: 10% of discretionary income; forgiveness after 20 years
  • IBR: 10-15% of discretionary income; forgiveness after 20-25 years
  • ICR: 20% of discretionary income; forgiveness after 25 years

Best For: Nurses with high loan balances relative to income, or those who don't qualify for other forgiveness programs.

Apply At: StudentAid.gov/IDR

Maximizing Your Loan Forgiveness Benefits

Strategies for Success:

  1. Start Early: Begin tracking qualifying payments for PSLF from your first job
  2. Submit ECF Forms Annually: Don't wait years to verify your employment
  3. Consider Combining Programs: Some nurses use NURSE Corps for immediate repayment, then PSLF for remaining balance
  4. Check Employer Eligibility: Use the PSLF Help Tool to verify your employer qualifies
  5. Stay Informed: Program rules and deadlines change; monitor official websites
  6. Keep Records: Save all payment confirmations and employment documentation
  7. Recertify Annually: Income-driven repayment plans require annual recertification
