California State University, Fullerton - RN to BSN Program

CSU Fullerton offers a flexible RN to BSN program designed for working nurses. The program is available in both online and hybrid formats, providing CCNE-accredited education that prepares registered nurses for leadership roles and advanced practice.

Location: Fullerton, CA
Format: Online & Hybrid
Accreditation: CCNE
Established: 1965

Program Features

  • CCNE accredited program ensuring quality education
  • Flexible online and hybrid formats for working nurses
  • Part-time and full-time enrollment options
  • Rolling admissions with multiple start dates per year
  • Experienced faculty with clinical expertise
  • Strong emphasis on evidence-based practice and research
  • Community health nursing practicum
  • Leadership and management coursework
  • Affordable tuition for California State University system

Admission Requirements

  • Current, unencumbered California RN license
  • Associate degree in nursing (ADN) from a regionally accredited institution
  • Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 (2.75 preferred)
  • Completion of CSU General Education requirements or IGETC
  • Prerequisites: Statistics course (with grade of C or better)
  • Official transcripts from all colleges attended
  • Personal statement describing career goals
  • Two professional references
  • Current CPR certification (BLS for Healthcare Providers)
  • Health clearances and immunizations
  • Criminal background check and drug screening

Curriculum

The 30-unit BSN program includes:

Core Nursing Courses (24 units)

  • NURS 300: Professional Role Transition (3 units)
  • NURS 310: Health Assessment (3 units)
  • NURS 320: Pathophysiology for Advanced Practice (3 units)
  • NURS 330: Nursing Research and Evidence-Based Practice (3 units)
  • NURS 340: Community and Public Health Nursing (3 units)
  • NURS 350: Nursing Leadership and Management (3 units)
  • NURS 360: Healthcare Policy and Ethics (3 units)
  • NURS 370: Community Health Practicum (3 units)

General Education Requirements (6 units)

  • Upper-division general education courses if not completed
  • Must meet CSU GE Breadth or IGETC requirements

Application Process

  1. Submit CSU Application: Apply through Cal State Apply
  2. Application Fee: $70 (non-refundable)
  3. Submit Official Transcripts: From all colleges and universities attended
  4. Complete Supplemental Application: Nursing-specific supplemental materials
  5. Submit Personal Statement: Describe your career goals and why you're pursuing your BSN
  6. Provide References: Two professional references (preferably from supervisors or nursing faculty)
  7. Upload RN License: Current, unencumbered California RN license
  8. Complete Background Check: Submitted through university portal

Financial Aid & Scholarships

Estimated Total Program Costs:

  • Tuition & Fees (In-State): $8,400
  • Tuition & Fees (Out-of-State): $11,880
  • Books & Supplies: $600-$800
  • Technology Fees: $150-$200
  • Clinical Expenses (background check, health clearances): $300-$500

Financial Aid Options

  • Federal Student Aid (FAFSA): Complete at studentaid.gov
  • Cal Grant: State grant for California residents
  • CSU System Scholarships: Various merit and need-based scholarships
  • CSUF Nursing Scholarships: Department-specific scholarships for nursing students
  • Employer Tuition Reimbursement: Many healthcare employers offer assistance
  • Veterans Benefits: GI Bill and other VA education benefits accepted
  • Nurse Corps Loan Repayment Program: Federal program for nurses in underserved areas

Career Outcomes

Graduates of the CSU Fullerton RN to BSN program are prepared for:

  • Leadership and management positions in healthcare
  • Clinical nurse specialist roles
  • Community and public health nursing
  • Case management positions
  • Quality improvement and patient safety roles
  • Graduate education (MSN, DNP, PhD programs)

Employment Rate: 95%+ of graduates employed in nursing within 6 months
