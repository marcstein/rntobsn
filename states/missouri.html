RN to BSN Programs in Missouri

Missouri offers 26+ accredited RN to BSN programs providing flexible pathways for registered nurses to earn their Bachelor of Science in Nursing degree. Programs are available across the state in cities including Kansas City, St. Louis, Springfield, with options for online, campus-based, and hybrid learning formats.

Total Programs: 26+
Online Programs: 20+
Cost Range: $305-$625/credit
Typical Duration: 12-24 months

About RN to BSN Programs in Missouri

RN to BSN programs in Missouri are designed for working registered nurses who want to advance their careers with a bachelor's degree. These programs typically require 30-60 credits beyond your ADN and can be completed in 12-24 months depending on your enrollment status.

Program Formats Available

  • Online Programs: Complete your degree entirely online with asynchronous coursework
  • Hybrid Programs: Combination of online classes with occasional on-campus requirements
  • Campus Programs: Traditional on-campus format with in-person instruction

Why Earn Your BSN in Missouri?

  • Access to leadership and management positions
  • Higher salary potential (BSN nurses earn 10-20% more on average)
  • Preparation for graduate education (MSN, DNP, PhD)
  • Meeting Magnet hospital requirements
  • Enhanced critical thinking and evidence-based practice skills

General Requirements for Missouri Programs

  • Active, unencumbered RN license in Missouri (or compact state)
  • Associate degree in nursing (ADN) or nursing diploma from an accredited program
  • Minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5-3.0 (varies by program)
  • Completion of general education requirements (if not met in ADN program)
  • Current CPR certification (BLS for Healthcare Providers)
  • Criminal background check and health clearances
  • Professional liability insurance
  • Some programs may require prerequisite courses in statistics or research

How to Choose an RN to BSN Program in Missouri

When selecting a program, consider the following factors:

  • Accreditation: Ensure the program is accredited by CCNE or ACEN
  • Cost: Compare total program costs including tuition, fees, and books
  • Format: Choose between online, hybrid, or campus-based delivery
  • Schedule: Consider full-time vs. part-time options and course start dates
  • Clinical Requirements: Understand local clinical placement requirements
  • Support Services: Look for academic advising, career services, and technical support
  • Transfer Credits: Verify how your ADN credits will transfer
  • Financial Aid: Explore scholarships, grants, and employer tuition assistance

Financial Aid and Scholarships

Funding options for RN to BSN students in Missouri include:

  • Federal Student Aid (FAFSA): Grants, loans, and work-study programs
  • State Grants and Scholarships: Various programs for Missouri residents
  • Nursing Scholarships: Professional organizations and foundations
  • Employer Tuition Assistance: Many healthcare facilities offer tuition reimbursement
  • Loan Forgiveness Programs: For nurses working in underserved areas
  • Veterans Benefits: GI Bill and other VA education benefits
  • Institutional Scholarships: Offered directly by universities

Career Outcomes

Earning your BSN in Missouri opens doors to:

  • Nursing leadership and management roles
  • Clinical nurse specialist positions
  • Community and public health nursing
  • Quality improvement and patient safety roles
  • Case management positions
  • Graduate nursing programs (MSN, DNP, PhD)
  • Nurse educator opportunities

Next Steps

  1. Research programs in Missouri and compare costs, formats, and requirements
  2. Contact admissions offices to request information and ask questions
