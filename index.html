About RN to BSN Programs

RN to BSN programs are designed for registered nurses who hold an Associate Degree in Nursing (ADN) or diploma in nursing and want to earn their Bachelor of Science in Nursing (BSN) degree. These programs build upon your existing nursing knowledge and clinical experience while providing advanced education in:

  • Leadership and Management
  • Community and Public Health
  • Evidence-Based Practice and Research
  • Healthcare Policy and Ethics
  • Advanced Pathophysiology and Pharmacology
  • Nursing Informatics

Why Earn Your BSN?

Access to management and leadership positions
BSN nurses earn 10-20% more on average
Required for MSN, DNP, and PhD programs
Many hospitals prefer or require BSN

Program Formats

Online Programs: Complete your degree entirely online with flexible scheduling. Perfect for working nurses.

Campus Programs: Traditional on-campus format with in-person classes and clinical experiences.

Hybrid Programs: Combination of online coursework and on-campus requirements.

Typical Requirements

  • Current, unencumbered RN license
  • Associate degree in nursing (ADN) or nursing diploma
  • Minimum GPA (typically 2.5-3.0)
  • Prerequisite courses (may vary by program)
  • Professional references
  • Personal statement or essay

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does an RN to BSN program take?

Most RN to BSN programs can be completed in 12-24 months of full-time study. Part-time options are available at many schools and may take 2-3 years. Accelerated programs can be completed in as little as 12 months.

How much does an RN to BSN program cost?

Tuition varies widely by institution. Public universities typically charge $250-$500 per credit hour, while private institutions may charge $400-$800+ per credit hour. Most programs require 30-60 credits beyond your ADN.

Can I work while completing my RN to BSN?

Yes! Most RN to BSN programs are designed for working nurses. Online and hybrid formats offer flexible scheduling, and many students continue working full-time while completing their degree.

What is the difference between CCNE and ACEN accreditation?

Both CCNE (Commission on Collegiate Nursing Education) and ACEN (Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing) are recognized accrediting bodies for nursing programs. Both types of accreditation are widely accepted by employers and graduate programs.

Will my ADN credits transfer?

Most RN to BSN programs accept your ADN nursing credits, though you may need to complete general education requirements if they weren't part of your ADN program. Each school evaluates transfer credits individually.

Are online RN to BSN programs legitimate?

Yes! Many reputable, accredited universities offer online RN to BSN programs. Always verify that a program is accredited by CCNE or ACEN before enrolling.
