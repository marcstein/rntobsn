What to Look for in an Online RN to BSN Program

  • Accreditation: Ensure the program is accredited by CCNE or ACEN
  • State Authorization: Verify the program is authorized to enroll students in your state
  • Clinical Requirements: Understand local clinical placement requirements and support
  • Course Format: Asynchronous (work at your own pace) vs. synchronous (scheduled class times)
  • Program Length: Full-time vs. part-time options and expected completion time
  • Technology Requirements: Ensure you have necessary equipment and internet access
  • Student Support: Academic advising, technical support, and library access
  • Total Cost: Tuition, fees, books, and additional expenses
  • Financial Aid: Availability of scholarships, grants, and payment plans
  • Transfer Credits: How many credits transfer from your ADN program

Frequently Asked Questions About Online Programs

Are online RN to BSN degrees as respected as campus-based degrees?

Yes! As long as the program is accredited by CCNE or ACEN, online BSN degrees are equally respected by employers and graduate schools. Your diploma will not indicate that you completed the program online.

Do online programs require any campus visits?

Most programs are 100% online with no campus visits required. Some hybrid programs may require occasional on-campus sessions. Clinical hours are typically completed in your local area.

How do clinical requirements work for online programs?

Most online RN to BSN programs require 80-150 clinical hours in community or public health settings. Many programs allow you to arrange your own clinical site with approval, or they may help you find placements in your area.

Can I take online classes while working full-time?

Yes! Online RN to BSN programs are specifically designed for working nurses. Most offer asynchronous courses, allowing you to complete coursework on your schedule.

How long does it take to complete an online RN to BSN?

Completion time varies based on enrollment status and program structure. Full-time students typically finish in 12-18 months, while part-time students may take 20-30 months. Accelerated programs can be completed in as little as 9-12 months.
